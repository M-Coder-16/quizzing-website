SET #1

A 1. According to Matthew, chapter 11, verse 18, what do they say?
A. "He has a demon." (Matthew 11:18)

G 2. When did Jesus go on from there to teach and preach in the towns of Galilee?
A. After he had finished instructing his twelve disciples (Matthew 11:1)

G 3. Where are those who wear fine clothes?
A. In kings' palaces (Matthew 11:8)

R 4. Finish these verses and give the reference: ""Come to me, all you. . ."
A. ". . .who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest. Take my yoke upon you and learn from me, for I am gentle and humble in heart, and you will find rest for your souls. For my yoke is easy and my burden is light." " (Matthew 11:28-30)

S 5. Situation question: who said it, to whom, and when: "Are you the one who is to come, or should we expect someone else?"
A. John's disciples said it to Jesus [OR the Messiah], when John heard about the deeds of the Messiah and sent his disciples (Matthew 11:2-3)

G 6. Who is the Elijah who was to come if you are willing to accept it?
A. John (Matthew 11:14)

Q 7. Quote Matthew, chapter 11, verses 28 through 30.
A. "Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest. Take my yoke upon you and learn from me, for I am gentle and humble in heart, and you will find rest for your souls. For my yoke is easy and my burden is light." (Matthew 11:28-30)

A. Remained to this day (Matthew 11:23)

G 9. To whom have all things been committed by his Father?
A. Jesus (Matthew 11:27)

V 10. Finish these verses: ""Come to me, all you. . ."
A. ". . .who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest. Take my yoke upon you and learn from me, for I am gentle and humble in heart, and you will find rest for your souls. For my yoke is easy and my burden is light." (Matthew 11:28-30)

A. Tyre and Sidon (Matthew 11:21)

A. When children played the pipe for you (Matthew 11:17)

A. Malachi (Matthew 11:10)

A. John's disciples (Matthew 11:4)

A. The towns in which most of Jesus' miracles had been performed (Matthew 11:20)

G 16. To whom has the Father revealed these things?
A. Little children (Matthew 11:25)

A 17. According to Matthew, chapter 11, verse 2, who was in prison?
A. John (Matthew 11:2)

A. A reed (Matthew 11:7)

A. For Jesus is gentle and humble in heart, and you will find rest for your souls (Matthew 11:29)

A. The Son of Man (Matthew 11:19)
