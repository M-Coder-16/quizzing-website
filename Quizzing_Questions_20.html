R 7. Finish this verse and give the reference: "just as the Son of. . ."
A. ". . .Man did not come to be served, but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many." " (Matthew 20:28)

S 8. Situation question: who said it, to whom, and what was the reply: "Grant that one of these two sons of mine may sit at your right and the other at your left in your kingdom."?
A. The mother of Zebedee's sons said it to Jesus, and the reply was, "You don't know what you are asking. Can you drink the cup I am going to drink?" (Matthew 20:21-22)

G 9. What did each one of them who were hired first also receive?
A. A denarius (Matthew 20:10)

X 10. In Matthew, chapter 20, how much was the usual daily wage of a day laborer?
A. A denarius (Matthew 20:2)

A 11. According to Matthew, chapter 20, verse 18, where are we going?
A. Up to Jerusalem (Matthew 20:18)

G 12. What did a landowner find about five in the afternoon?
A. Still others standing around (Matthew 20:6)

A 13. According to Matthew, chapter 20, verse 23, what did Jesus say to them?
A. "You will indeed drink from my cup, but to sit at my right or left is not for me to grant. These places belong to those for whom they have been prepared by my Father." (Matthew 20:23)

V 14. Finish this verse: "just as the Son of. . ."
A. ". . .Man did not come to be served, but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many." (Matthew 20:28)

G 15. What has the landowner made these who were hired last?
A. Equal to us who have borne the burden of the work and the heat of the day (Matthew 20:12)

G 16. For what did a landowner hire workers?
A. His vineyard (Matthew 20:1)

A 17. According to Matthew, chapter 20, verse 20, who asked a favor of Jesus?
A. The mother of Zebedee's sons (Matthew 20:20)

G 18. Who worked only one hour?
A. These who were hired last (Matthew 20:12)

G 19. For whom did the Son of Man come to give his life as a ransom?
A. Many (Matthew 20:28)

G 20. Who shouted, "Lord, Son of David, have mercy on us!"?
A. Two blind men (Matthew 20:30)
