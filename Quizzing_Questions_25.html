Matthew 25

SET #1

A 1. According to Matthew, chapter 25, verse 23, what did his master reply?
A. "Well done, good and faithful servant! You have been faithful with a few things; I will put you in charge of many things. Come and share your master's happiness!" (Matthew 25:23)

X 2. Two-part question: in Matthew, chapter 25, who were foolish, and who were wise?
A. 1) Five of the virgins who took their lamps and went out to meet the bridegroom; 2) Five of the virgins who took their lamps and went out to meet the bridegroom (Matthew 25:2)

G 3. Whose money did the man who had received one bag hide?
A. His master's (Matthew 25:18)

S 4. Situation question: who said it, and to whom: "Master, you entrusted me with five bags of gold. See, I have gained five more."?
A. The man who had received five bags of gold said it to his master (Matthew 25:20)

G 5. Who returned and settled accounts with them?
A. The master of those servants (Matthew 25:19)

R 6. Finish this verse and give the reference: ""When the Son of Man. . ."
A. ". . .comes in his glory, and all the angels with him, he will sit on his glorious throne. " (Matthew 25:31)

A 7. According to Matthew, chapter 25, verse 27, what should you have done?
A. Put my money on deposit with the bankers (Matthew 25:27)

G 8. Where should that worthless servant be thrown?
A. Outside, into the darkness (where there will be weeping and gnashing of teeth) (Matthew 25:30)

G 9. Where will the Son of Man put the sheep?
A. On his right (Matthew 25:33)

V 10. Finish these verses: "All the nations will be. . ."
A. ". . .gathered before him, and he will separate the people one from another as a shepherd separates the sheep from the goats. He will put the sheep on his right and the goats on his left." (Matthew 25:32-33)

G 11. Who put his money to work and gained five bags more?
A. The man who had received five bags of gold (Matthew 25:16)

A 12. According to Matthew, chapter 25, verse 9, what did they reply?
A. "No, there may not be enough for both us and you. Instead, go to those who sell oil and buy some for yourselves." (Matthew 25:9)

G 13. What is prepared for the devil and his angels?
A. The eternal fire (Matthew 25:41)

Q 14. Quote Matthew, chapter 25, verse 35.
A. "For I was hungry and you gave me something to eat, I was thirsty and you gave me something to drink, I was a stranger and you invited me in," (Matthew 25:35)

G 15. Whom will the righteous answer, "Lord, when did we see you hungry and feed you, or thirsty and give you something to drink?"
A. The King [OR the Son of Man, OR the Lord] (Matthew 25:37)

G 16. Who will say to those on his right, "Come, you who are blessed by my Father; take your inheritance, the kingdom prepared for you since the creation of the world."?
A. The King [OR the Son of Man, OR the Lord] (Matthew 25:34)

A 17. According to Matthew, chapter 25, verse 36, what did you do when I needed clothes?
A. You clothed me (Matthew 25:36)

G 18. When did those on the King's left give the King nothing to eat?
A. When he was hungry (Matthew 25:42)

G 19. Who did for the King whatever they did for one of the least of these brothers and sisters of his?
A. The righteous [OR those on the King's right] (Matthew 25:40)

G 20. Who will reply, "Truly I tell you, whatever you did not do for one of the least of these, you did not do for me."?
A. The King [OR the Son of Man, OR the Lord] (Matthew 25:45)

SET #10

A 1. According to Matthew, chapter 25, verse 46, who will go away to eternal life?
A. The righteous (Matthew 25:46)

G 2. Whom will the righteous answer, "Lord, when did we see you hungry and feed you, or thirsty and give you something to drink?"
A. The King [OR the Son of Man, OR the Lord] (Matthew 25:37)

A 3. According to Matthew, chapter 25, verse 40, what did you do for me?
A. Whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers and sisters of mine (Matthew 25:40)

G 4. What should the wicked, lazy servant have put on deposit with the bankers?
A. His master's money (Matthew 25:27)

G 5. What is prepared for the devil and his angels?
A. The eternal fire (Matthew 25:41)

Q 6. Quote Matthew, chapter 25, verses 32 through 33.
A. "All the nations will be gathered before him, and he will separate the people one from another as a shepherd separates the sheep from the goats. He will put the sheep on his right and the goats on his left." (Matthew 25:32-33)

S 7. Situation question: who will say it, and to whom: "Come, you who are blessed by my Father; take your inheritance, the kingdom prepared for you since the creation of the world."?
A. The King [OR the Son of Man, OR the Lord] will say it to those on his right (Matthew 25:34)

G 8. Why was the man who had received one bag of gold afraid?
A. Because he knew that his master is a hard man, harvesting where he has not sown and gathering where he has not scattered seed (Matthew 25:24-25)

G 9. Where will the Son of Man put the sheep?
A. On his right (Matthew 25:33)

R 10. Finish this verse and give the reference: ""When the Son of Man. . ."
A. ". . .comes in his glory, and all the angels with him, he will sit on his glorious throne. " (Matthew 25:31)

X 11. Two-part question: who did not take any oil with them, and who took oil in jars along with their lamps?
A. 1) The foolish virgins; 2) The wise virgins (Matthew 25:3-4)

G 12. How many virgins who took their lamps and went out to meet the bridegroom were foolish?
A. Five (Matthew 25:2)

A 13. According to Matthew, chapter 25, verse 43, when did you not clothe me?
A. When I needed clothes (Matthew 25:43)

G 14. Of what will his master put the servant who has gained two more bags of gold in charge?
A. Many things (Matthew 25:23)

G 15. Who replied, "Well done, good and faithful servant! You have been faithful with a few things; I will put you in charge of many things. Come and share your master's happiness!"?
A. The servant's master (Matthew 25:21)

A 16. According to Matthew, chapter 25, verse 42, when did you give me nothing to drink?
A. When I was thirsty (Matthew 25:42)

V 17. Finish this verse: "For I was hungry and. . ."

G 18. Whose lamps are going out?
A. The foolish virgins' lamps (Matthew 25:8)
